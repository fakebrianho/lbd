Registration

01 / Four questions

The tournament is invite only, with the goal of creating a space for Chinese Heritage Churches to fellowship and evangelize. The tournament is for players aged 13 and older across basketball, volleyball, pickleball, and table tennis. Roster sizes vary by sport — details are listed on the sign-up page for each.
Mixed-gender teams are welcome in all sports. Currently rostered professional players are not eligible in their sport; former pros may only compete in the highest division of their respective sport once two full seasons have passed since their last contract.

Team entry is $250 flat, regardless of roster size. This covers court fees, referees, uniforms, and post-tournament gear for every player on your roster.

Limited financial assistance is available — email us if cost is a barrier and we'll work something out.

Registration closes at 11:59 PM on August 15, 2026. Spots within each division are first come, first served, so don't wait.

Currently only ping pong and pickleball accept individual sign-ups.

Tournament Format

02 / Three questions

TBoth basketball and volleyball consist of pool play to determine seeding and single-elimination playoffs.

Pickleball and Ping Pong additionally offer recreational (non-competitive) play time as well.

Basketball and volleyball play outdoors at Grant Park, Humboldt Park, and Garfield Park. Pickleball and table tennis are held at nearby indoor facilities. Venue assignments are sent with your bracket draw on July 1.

Each sport crowns its own champions across open and veterans divisions. Prizes and awards vary by bracket — full details are shared when registration closes.

Rules & Play

03 / Three questions

Rules vary by sport. Basketball is full-court 5-on-5 with running-clock halves. Volleyball follows standard rally scoring. Pickleball and table tennis use best-of-three match formats. Full rule sheets for each sport are published before the tournament.

Certified officials are provided for every game, paid by the tournament. Basketball and volleyball games have referees; pickleball and table tennis matches have designated judges.

Tournament-issued gear is required for all games and is included in your entry fee. You'll be assigned colors when your team is confirmed. Sponsor patches up to four inches are allowed.

Spectators

04 / One question

All outdoor games are free and open to the public. Indoor pickleball and table tennis finals may require tickets — details posted closer to the event.
