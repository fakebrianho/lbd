Registration

01 / Four questions
The tournament is invite only, with the goal of creating a space for Chinese Heritage Churches to fellowship and evangelize. The tournament is for players aged 13 and older across basketball, volleyball, pickleball, and table tennis. Roster sizes vary by sport — details are listed on the sign-up page for each. Mixed-gender teams are welcome in all sports.
Currently rostered professional players, Pro-Amateur, NCAA, or equivalent level athletes are eligible to play but must be regular attendees of the represented Church team. (Not Guests) Their team must play in the highest division of their respective sport.

Pickleball - $55 per person. ($50 early-bird)

Ping Pong - $20 per person. ($10 early-bird)

Volleyball - $400 per team. ($350 early-bird)

Basketball - $475 per team. ($425 early-bird)

Registration closes at 11:59 PM on Sunday August 23rd, 2026. Spots within each division are first come, first served, so don't wait.

Early bird registration deadline is 11:59PM August 9th, 2026

Currently only ping pong and pickleball accept individual sign-ups.

Tournament Format

02 / Three questions

Both basketball and volleyball consist of pool play to determine seeding and single-elimination playoffs.

Pickleball and Ping Pong additionally offer recreational (non-competitive) play time as well.

Basketball and volleyball play Hudson Sportsplex, 31W290 Schoger Dr, Naperville, IL 60564. Pickleball and table tennis are held at nearby indoor facility at Pickled!, 7250 Woodward Ave, Woodridge, IL 60517.

Each sport crowns its own champions across each division.

Rules & Play

03 / Three questions

Rules vary by sport. Basketball is full-court 5-on-5 with running-clock halves. Volleyball follows standard rally scoring. Pickleball and table tennis use best-of-three match formats. Full rule sheets for each sport are published before the tournament.

Basketball and volleyball games have referees; pickleball and table tennis matches have self judged.

Yes, teams are asked to bring their own jerseys / uniforms. They do NOT need to professionally made, but please make sure the dominant color or aesthetic matches in a way where it's clear that you're a single team.

For basketball specfically:

To ensure opposing teams are wearing different colors, teams should either have MULTIPLE alternative colored jerseys or reversible jerseys.

Spectators

04 / One question

All outdoor games are free and open to the public. Indoor pickleball and table tennis finals may require tickets — details posted closer to the event.
